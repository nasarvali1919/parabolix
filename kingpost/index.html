Step-by-Step Procedure

Goal: Align upper bearing (Leg 3) with lower bearing (kingpost base) so they share the same vertical rotation axis. When aligned → bubble stays still during azimuth rotation.
Prerequisites
• Full reflector weight installed
• Rough-leveled with spirit level (within 0.5°)
• Lower pintle grouted and cured
• Measurements at night (thermally stable)
• Anchor nuts snug, push bolts greased
Step 1 — Place Level
Place precision bubble level on the kingpost tube (flat/machined surface).
Orient it so it reads tilt in the leg direction (North-South if legs are North).
Step 2 — Read Center Position
Position antenna at center of rotation (0° azimuth).
Read bubble → Record as Center (usually set as 0 reference).
Step 3 — Read at Leg 1
Rotate antenna 45° toward Leg 1 side.
Read bubble offset → Record as Leg 1 reading.
Note direction: + if bubble moved toward legs, − if away.
Step 4 — Read at Leg 2
Return to center, then rotate 45° toward Leg 2 side.
Read bubble offset → Record as Leg 2 reading.
Step 5 — Calculate & Adjust
Enter readings in Measure tab → app tells you what to adjust.
Always return to center before adjusting!
Step 6 — Repeat
Re-measure after each adjustment.
Repeat until bubble stays still (within spec) during full rotation.
Adjustment Logic
Scenario A: Bubble moves toward Leg 1 when at Leg 1 position
→ Upper bearing tilts toward Leg 1
→ Fix: Raise Leg 1 (tighten anchor nut) OR lower Leg 3 on Leg 1 side
Scenario B: Bubble moves toward Leg 2 when at Leg 2 position
→ Upper bearing tilts toward Leg 2
→ Fix: Raise Leg 2 (tighten anchor nut) OR lower Leg 3 on Leg 2 side
Scenario C: Bubble moves same direction at both positions
→ Whole kingpost leans one way
→ Fix: Adjust Leg 3 (upper bearing leg) to shift the upper bearing back to center
Scenario D: Bubble moves opposite directions at Leg 1 vs Leg 2
→ Classic lean — one side high, other low
→ Fix: Raise the low side leg, lower the high side leg
Remember: Leg 3 acts as a seesaw — lowering its left side raises the right side and vice versa. Use Leg 3 for fine East-West correction of the upper bearing position.

Formulas & Specifications

Bubble → Angle Conversion
Tilt (arcsec) = divisions × sensitivity (arcsec/div) Tilt (degrees) = arcsec / 3600 Tilt (in/in) = degrees / 57.3
Error Calculation
Δ(Center − Leg1) = |rC − rL1| × sensitivity Δ(Center − Leg2) = |rC − rL2| × sensitivity Δ(Leg1 − Leg2) = |rL1 − rL2| × sensitivity Max Peak Error = max of above three (arcsec) Lean angle = Max Peak Error / 3600 (degrees)
Specifications (from GD 400-0429B)
Standard kingpost: ≤ 0.030° = 108 arcsec = 0.0005 in/in TT&C / Critical: ≤ 0.010° = 36 arcsec = 0.00018 in/in Equivalent in divisions: At 20"/div: Standard = 5.4 div, TT&C = 1.8 div At 10"/div: Standard = 10.8 div, TT&C = 3.6 div At 42"/div: Standard = 2.6 div, TT&C = 0.86 div
GD Theodolite Method (for reference)
Error = P / R × 57.3 (degrees) P = max peak scale reading difference (inches) R = radius from AZ axis to scale (inches) Equivalent: 0.030° at R=50" → P = 0.025" max
