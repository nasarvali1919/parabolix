📐 Dial Gauge Setup Guide

Elevation Axis Setup

View: Standing behind the dish, looking at the resolver coupling from the back.

A = Horizontal gauge (plunger points toward coupling)
B = Vertical gauge (plunger points up toward coupling)

CW rotation: As seen from behind the dish = dish moves UP in elevation.

Azimuth Axis Setup

View: Looking DOWN at the resolver coupling from above.

A = First gauge that shows MAX deflection on CW rotation
B = The other gauge reading at same rotation

CW rotation: As seen from above = antenna rotates clockwise (like a clock on the ground).

Universal A/B Rule

Rotate CW by one pin spacing (Q°).
A = gauge with LARGER deflection
B = gauge with SMALLER deflection

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Mount both gauges at 90° to each other on the resolver coupling
  2. Zero both gauges
  3. Jog the axis CW (from your defined viewpoint) by one pin spacing
  4. Read both gauges
  5. A = the one that moved MORE
  6. B = the one that moved LESS
  7. Record the difference (after − before) with sign

Sign Convention

  • Positive (+) = needle moved CW on the dial face (gauge compressed / plunger pushed in)
  • Negative (−) = needle moved CCW on the dial face (gauge extended / plunger pulled out)
⚠️ CW/CCW View Confusion:
Always define CW from ONE viewpoint:
• EL axis: Stand behind dish → CW = dish goes UP
• AZ axis: Look down from above → CW = clockwise on ground

If you look from the opposite side, CW appears as CCW. Pick one side and stick with it.
